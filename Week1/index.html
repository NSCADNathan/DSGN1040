Atomic Structure

HOW?

Atoms are the building blocks of the physical world. They are made of a centre (nucleus), surrounded by a negatively charged cloud of electrons.

Being so negative, electrons look for any excuse to get away. They move from atom to atom as space opens up for them.

Common thinking: Bohr's model — electrons orbit like planets. Reality: a dense cloud of probability.

In Particular

A Lightbulb

A lightbulb has a small wire running through it — the filament. The leads coming into it are much thicker.

The thinner filament causes friction with the electrons. The intense resistance causes heat, which gives off radiant energy running from visible light into IR.

It doesn't burst into flames because there is no oxygen in the bulb — only inert gases (argon and nitrogen).

Common Problem

Short Circuits

It's also possible the thing IS connected, but electricity is finding a route around it — this is called a short circuit.

Like most of us, electricity will always take the easiest route. The path of least resistance.

But how does electricity actually move in a circuit?

Which way would YOU rather go?

Electricity will ALWAYS find the shortest route to complete the circuit, even (or maybe especially) if it means bypassing the loads in a circuit.

Components

Bits + Pieces

  • We know current flow requires a pathway.
  • This pathway is usually made of highly conductive material.
  • The opposite of conducting is insulating — this impedes current flow.
  • A wire is a highly conductive material encased in an insulator.
  • Copper, Silver, Gold — all the junk we make jewellery from makes great conductors!

Components

Bits N Pieces

Like Lego, but more electrocutiony

Switches Open or close electrical pathways. Some have multiple states.
Resistors Reduce or redirect current flow precisely. Colour bands indicate value. Not polarized.
Batteries Store and provide running electricity. Always polarized.
Capacitors Store electricity. Some, but not all, polarized.
Diodes Allow current to pass in one direction only. Cover a huge range of functions including signal transmission, voltage protection and regulation.
DIODES!!
